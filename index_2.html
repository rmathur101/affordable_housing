Here are some ideas...
1) compare districts by level of funding, number of affordable units, and opportunity for income groups

2) assess housing developers on usage of city funds and development of affordable units

3) discover locations of affordable housing projects along with specific details about funding, status, and opportunity

4) find affordable housing that is compatible with yearly income

5) make good investment decisions about where city funds go to maximize affordable housing that creates opportunity and serves a variety of income groups
The data comes from the City of Austin. You can find it: here. There are known issues with the data quality. For example, do districts 6 and 10 really not have ANY affordable housing right now? We don't know.

We want to work with the city to improve the integrity of this data. We would connect this application to a real time database of affordable housing inventory. This would allow our city to gain real time insights and provide austinites with affordable housing they can rely on.
